For my second-year paper, I plan to investigate the interaction of downward nominal wage rigidities and the zero-lower bound.
The zero-lower bound on nominal interest rates bound has seen renewed attention since central banks in advanced countries cut their policy rates nearly to zero with the recent recession.

A world where the zero lower bound binds is likely to be a world where inflation is low.  This is also a world where downward wage rigidities will likely bind as well.

I intentionally used interaction, in the sense of each having an effect on the other.  The central bank might be interested in adjusted it's policy (target rate of inflation, say) if it must worry about DNWR and its effects on the economy.  Also, the welfare loss (or gain) of DNWR will depend on the central banks behavior, which my be constrained by the ZLB.

Prior research ---e.g. \cite{coibon_gorodnichenko_wieland_2012}--- has looked at optimal monetary policy in light of the ZLB with a simple version of DNWR added as an extension.  
They found that DNWR actually were welfare-improving by lower the variance of inflation and output.
However, recent research on DNWR ---e.g. by \cite{benigno_ricci_2011}--- has emphasized the importance of idiosyncratic shocks (rather than homogeneous as in \cite{coibon_gorodnichenko_wieland_2012}) in generating effects at the aggregate level.
